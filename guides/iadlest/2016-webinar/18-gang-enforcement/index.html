Analysis to Support Gang Enforcement

This presentation outlines how the Wilmington, NC Police Department used data analysis and interagency collaboration to combat a surge in gang-related violence beginning around 2012. It highlights the critical role of intelligence-led policing, community engagement, and strategic enforcement in restoring public safety.

Link to the original webinar on vimeo.com:

Using Analysis to Support Gang Enforcement

Presentation on Gang Task Force in Wilmington, NC (2018)

Section titled “Presentation on Gang Task Force in Wilmington, NC (2018)”
  1. Gang problems across the country are “monolithic.” This presentation covers Wilmington specifically.
  2. Many gangs in Wilmington are structured:
    • Five-star shot callers in prison communicate with members on the street.
  3. Population: 118,000 across 41 square miles.
  4. Wilmington is considered a “two-hop” city.
  5. Wilmington Police Department: 278 officers
    • Multiple Federal Task Force relationships:
      • Two Task Force Officers on each: DEA, US Marshals, FBI Safe Streets, ATF
    • Close partnership with New Hanover County Sheriff’s Office
    • Includes narcotics, uniformed “jump out” units, and plainclothes surveillance
    • Pay & benefits did not retain hires, prompting city leaders for support

Gang Profile

Section titled “Gang Profile”
  • United Blood Nation
  • Crips

Contributing factors:

  • Concentrated in public housing
  • Violence, easy access to guns (due to weak local gun laws)
  • Heroin: large profit incentives
    • Hospitals changed opioid distribution policies without police awareness
    • ER doctors indirectly facilitated addiction
  • Female “enablers”
    • Drove cars, transported gang members/drugs—overlooked by police
  • Josh Prouty murder
    • Led to creation of nonprofit Journey 4 Josh (J4J)
    • This tragedy catalyzed significant policy changes

The Rising NC Problem Used GangNet

Section titled “The Rising NC Problem Used GangNet”

GangNet is a database system used by law enforcement to track gang members and associates.

— https://search.brave.com/search?q=What+is+GangNet

  • Violent gangs mapped with GIS
  • Wilmington had highest crime rate per geographic area as a major port city

2013 Explosive Spike in Violence

Section titled “2013 Explosive Spike in Violence”

Continue watching at 36m 49s

  • A leaked witness list—allegedly due to federal discovery mishandling—was used as a kill list
  • Near-nightly drive-by shootings followed
  • Public housing mismanagement during renovation (possibly gentrification-related)
  • Poor accountability, female enablers, and rampant violence—Creekwood became a “battle zone”
  • High unemployment and no historical area revitalization

If you have a severe gang problem, use the ShotSpotter system.

ShotSpotter is a gunshot detection system using acoustic sensors to alert law enforcement in real time.
— https://search.brave.com/search?q=What+is+ShotSpotter

  • Reentry support was vital: employment to reduce recidivism
  • Overall crime down 40% since 1990s (Presenter questioned the data’s validity)

Community Violence

Section titled “Community Violence”
  • LE and public housing officials received threats; one attempted murder (FBI Safe Streets officer)
  • Tourniquets saved the life of FBI officer Mike Spencer
  • Key informants relocated for safety

Public Housing Crisis

Section titled “Public Housing Crisis”
  • Director fired
  • Gangs dominated complexes
  • Officials feared eviction enforcement
  • Corrupt site managers and community non-cooperation

This may justify National Guard intervention, due to local apathy and lawlessness.

The Crisis Exposed

Section titled “The Crisis Exposed”
  • Police were “blind”:
    • Unaware of gang memberships, conflicts, or territorial control
  • No centralized intelligence or coordinated gang response

Only One Crime Analyst

Section titled “Only One Crime Analyst”
  • Limited to hot spot patrol support
  • Tasked with gangs, public housing, and repeat offenders
  • Culture of internal information silos

Resource strain led to a “whack-a-mole” policing approach.

Launched Intel Process

Section titled “Launched Intel Process”
  • One Crime Analyst hired, began:
    • Centralizing and disseminating actionable intelligence
    • Building internal relationships
  • Analyst prioritized critical thinking over credentials

“We want actionable reports.”

  • Introduced 5WH (Who, What, When, Where, Why, How) as foundation of gang intel process

2016 ShotSpotter in Wilmington, NC

Section titled “2016 ShotSpotter in Wilmington, NC”
  • Most gunfire in northeast area—Creekwood

  • Weekly CompStat-style meetings:
    • Patrol, Jump Out Squads, Narcotics, CID, Parole & Probation
    • ShotSpotter data and 5WH analysis used

Getting Information with Technology

Section titled “Getting Information with Technology”

Suggestions:

  • Test anonymous tips
  • “Snap trends” (early warning system)—OSINT used proactively
  • Electronic monitoring (signed MOU with Community Corrections)
    • Used strategically and in real-time

Community trust improved and intel sharing increased.

Crime Analysts dropped into chaotic gang environments face significant organizational challenges.

More Gathering of Information

Section titled “More Gathering of Information”

  • Group texting (real-time info sharing)

  • Wednesday meetings (some resistance from Parole/Probation)

  • Regional intelligence reports

  • Yapper (Police-only social platform)

  • Outcome: Enabled Housing Unit Task Force—true COPS (Community-Oriented Policing Strategy)

Centralizing Information

Section titled “Centralizing Information”
  • Intelligence flowed to Gang Unit and Gang Analyst:
    • Gang validations, joint operations with parole/probation, officer/community education
    • ALE (Alcohol Law Enforcement) involvement, warrantless searches
    • Deconflict intelligence, prioritize targets, foster regional awareness

Blueprint for effective gang/housing unit intel gathering.

  • Targeted arrests reduced northeast Wilmington gang violence

Sharing Information

Section titled “Sharing Information”
  • Weekly meetings addressed current threats
  • Delivered:
    • List of targets and responsible enforcement units
    • Summary report emailed to attendees

Communication Process

Section titled “Communication Process”

All info passed through Crime Analyst and flowed to/from:

  • ATF
  • CID
  • DEA
  • FBI Safe Streets
  • NHC Gang Unit
  • USAO
  • USMS

Presenter questioned CID’s integration into sensitive internal threads.

Intelligence-Led Policing (ILP) Strategy

Section titled “Intelligence-Led Policing (ILP) Strategy”

USAO agreed to:

  • RICO (Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act)
  • CCE (Continuing Criminal Enterprise)
  • Focus on female “enablers”
  • Add prosecutors
  • Gang validation and DA Office “Red Files” initiated

Goal: Interrupt retaliatory gang violence cycle

What is the Criminal Enterprise Approach?

  • Prosecute entire criminal organizations using RICO or CCE
  • Leaders held responsible
  • Enhanced penalties, asset seizure
  • Link members through conspiracy charges

Community Engaged ILP

Section titled “Community Engaged ILP”
  • Gang prevention specialists
  • Community-police collaboration
  • School Resource Officers
  • Public housing events and outreach

Trust must be earned by the Police.

Housing Strategy

Section titled “Housing Strategy”

Public Housing Authority → Police Dept:

  • Compare city crime benchmarks
  • Coordinate with NHSO (New Hanover Social Services)
  • Share intel with Crime Analyst
  • Implement housing improvement plans
  • 24/7 support on-site
  • Analysts aid housing officers in eviction and lease violations

Other Responses

Section titled “Other Responses”
  • Department-wide gang training and certifications
  • Engage with local schools
  • Encourage volunteerism
  • Support youth/adult programs

Public Housing Now

Section titled “Public Housing Now”
  • Crime dropped by over 50%
  • Started Cub Scout troop
  • Residents self-regulate
  • High expectations established

Current Process (Wilmington, NC)

Section titled “Current Process (Wilmington, NC)”
  • Gang injunction:
    • Affidavit authored by Gang Crime Analyst
  • RTCC Sting Center:
    • 21-hour coverage, regional expansion planned
    • Officer safety, hybrid command structure

Long-Term Solutions

Section titled “Long-Term Solutions”
  • Economic development:
    • Jobs, white-collar employers, revitalization, code enforcement

Success metrics include lower unemployment and commercial vacancy.

Ensure CompStat principles are applied.
