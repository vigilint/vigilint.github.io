Three Critical Steps: Roadmap

In this session, Law Enforcement Strategist Dawn Reeby presents a structured, intentional approach to personal and professional growth in crime analysis. By establishing Daily Modes of Operation (DMOs), analysts can align their work with agency goals, sharpen their skills, and move from reactive tasks to proactive, high-impact contributions.

Link to the original webinar on vimeo.com:

Three Critical Steps for Law Enforcement Analysts to Create a Road Map to Success

1. Daily Modes of Operation (DMO)

Section titled “1. Daily Modes of Operation (DMO)”
  • Why we create a roadmap to success: Motive, potential, confidence
  • Roadmaps help us adopt a proactive mindset for growth

2. Set Your Intention (Career, Education, Attitude)

Section titled “2. Set Your Intention (Career, Education, Attitude)”
  • SMART: Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Relevant, Time-Bound
  • Effectively serve my department (e.g., apprehend criminals or prevent crime)
  • Obtain highly marketable CA skills (certifications, professional development)

3. Create a Mindset (DMOs)

Section titled “3. Create a Mindset (DMOs)”
  • Establish daily habits to develop a proactive mind for analytics
  • Eliminate procrastination
  • Clarify steps, results, and timelines

Effectively Serve Your Department

Section titled “Effectively Serve Your Department”

Chart uses a mock hourly schedule.

Apprehend

Section titled “Apprehend”
  • Build rapport with detectives/patrol; attend roll call
  • Maintain searchable systems (1 hour)

Analyze

Section titled “Analyze”
  • Read reports and regional information daily (1 hour)
  • Analyze said reports (2 hours)

Develop Responses

Section titled “Develop Responses”
  • Produce actionable analytical reports (1 hour)
  • Identify potential responses (1 hour)

Evaluate

Section titled “Evaluate”
  • Evaluate the effectiveness of responses (1 hour per week)

Obtain Marketable CA Skills

Section titled “Obtain Marketable CA Skills”

1. CLEA Certification

Section titled “1. CLEA Certification”
  • Online certification plan
  • Dedicate 2 hours per week to certification work

2. Stay Updated on Tools

Section titled “2. Stay Updated on Tools”
  • Spend 1 hour reviewing IACA notices and tools
  • Attend local monthly training

3. Learn Advanced Access and Mapping

Section titled “3. Learn Advanced Access and Mapping”
  • Spend 1 hour a day increasing marketable skills

Step into the role and accomplish much—work effectively for your agency.
Break away from simply reacting; set up a schedule.

Create Your DMO (Sample Plan)

Section titled “Create Your DMO (Sample Plan)”
  1. Read daily reports (1 hour)
  2. Review regional bulletins (30 minutes)
  3. Analyze MV (Motor Vehicle) series (1 hour)
  4. Identify suspects (1 hour)
  5. Produce actionable report on MV series (1 hour)
  6. Attend 7 AM and 3 PM roll calls (30 minutes)
  7. Engage in personal growth activities (1 hour)
  8. Review emails/requests (1 hour)

Do the ASAP work first, then stick to a schedule to stay productive.
Eliminate tasks that don’t support daily DMOs.

Questions

Section titled “Questions”
  1. How can we tactfully assure a person’s request will be completed?
  • Give them an alotted time frame to complete the task
  1. Cleaning & sanitizing data is not held to quality standard, how do we proceed?
  • Crime Analysts are not data entry specialists (Records Unit)
  • Train executives on data-driven strategies (data quality)
  • Identify five key issues that are interrupting productivity (then make action plan)
  • Establish a cost analysis and what dollar amount cleaning & sanitizing data is costing
  • Focus on solutions, rather than problems
    • Drop down menu rather than text field
    • NIBRS codes compliance
    • Accountabilty in cleaning data
  • Give solutions to executives
